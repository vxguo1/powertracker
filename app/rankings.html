How this is computed

Campus list. Hand-curated from press releases, FOIA filings, local zoning records and news coverage. Every site carries a source URL in data/sites/data_centers.csv. "Announced load" is the publicly disclosed megawatt figure where the operator or utility has stated one; many campuses (especially older hyperscaler builds) have never published a number and contribute zero to the MW total even though they show up in the campus count.

County rollup. Each campus is mapped from its city/state to a Census FIPS code via us_cities.csv + manual overrides for townships and unincorporated sites. Counties are then ranked by the sum of announced megawatts across all campuses they host.

Resi rate Δ. The percent change in the county's lead utility's residential electricity price between 2024 and the mean of 2021–2023, from EIA Form 861. Captures whether households in the county are already paying more for power on a multi-year baseline.

Home price Δ. Percent change in the county's trailing-3-month median sale price (volume-weighted by HOMES_SOLD) against the mean of the prior three same-month windows at t−12 / t−24 / t−36 months. From Redfin Data Center. Counties with < 30 sales in any of those four windows are dropped to no-data.

Property tax Δ. Percent change in the county's median real-estate tax (Census ACS B25103) between the latest 5-year endpoint and the mean of the prior three. ACS 5-year windows overlap by 4 years — treat magnitude as suggestive.

None of these Δs are caused by data centers alone. They are read-outs of what the host community has been paying for electricity and housing in the years the campus was being planned and built — the context that's missing from most press-release coverage.

Rankings rebuilt 2026-05-17 from all data sources. Map, campus list, refresh workflows and full pipeline are open source at github.com/vxguo1/powertracker. Every row in the campus list carries a citation URL.

Found a missing site, a wrong number, or want to add a layer? File an issue or a PR. The schema is open.
