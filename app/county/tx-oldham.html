Key takeaways

  • Oldham County has 2,097 residents, the smallest county in this batch. Hut 8's 205 MW Vega campus is roughly 98 kW of compute per resident, one of the highest per-capita ratios in the dataset.Source: Census ACS 2023 B01003 (Oldham County, TX) + app/county/tx-oldham.html
  • Vega sits on historic Route 66 and the surrounding county is wheat and wind. Oldham County hosts several utility-scale wind farms tied to the Xcel SPS grid, the same grid Hut 8 is now drawing 205 MW from.Source: sources[0] (Wikipedia: Oldham County, Texas)
  • Xcel SPS residential rates actually fell 0.1% from 2021 to 2023 and Oldham property tax assessments fell 4.4% over the same window. Hut 8's load has not yet flowed through to either bill.Source: data/cache/utility_rate_yoy.csv (Southwestern Public Service Co, TX) + data/cache/property_tax_yoy.csv (FIPS 48359)
  • Median household income in Oldham County is $76,402, the highest of the four West Texas counties in this batch. That income is propped up by a small population of ranch landowners rather than wage workers.Source: Census ACS 2023 B19013, B25077 (FIPS 48359)

Local socioeconomics

  • Only 0.7% of Oldham County's labor force is officially unemployed, on a working population of fewer than 800 adults. The Hut 8 Vega campus is landing in the tightest labor market in batch 4 by raw numbers.Source: Census ACS 2023 B23025 (FIPS 48359)
  • 11.3% of Oldham's workers are in manufacturing, second only to Nolan in West Texas batch 4. The Vega industrial corridor along I-40 has absorbed wind-tower and pipeline fabrication for two decades.Source: Census ACS 2023 C24050 (FIPS 48359)
  • 24.5% of Oldham residents are Hispanic and 9.8% of adults are veterans, the highest vet share of any rural Texas county in batch 4. Amarillo Air Force Base separations and Pantex retirees feed the local civilian base.Source: Census ACS 2023 B03003, B21001 (FIPS 48359)
  • 17.7% of Oldham residents live in poverty despite the $76,402 median income, the second highest poverty rate in batch 4. The ranching-landowner income concentration leaves the wage-worker third of the county exposed.Source: Census ACS 2023 B17001 (FIPS 48359)
